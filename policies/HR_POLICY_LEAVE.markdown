# **HR POLICY: LEAVE POLICIES**

## **Page 1: Introduction & General Principles**

### **1.1 Purpose**

This Leave Policy outlines the types of leave available to employees and the procedures for applying and approving leave. The Company is committed to supporting employee well-being, work-life balance, and compliance with statutory requirements.

### **1.2 Scope**

This policy applies to:

- Full-time employees
- Part-time employees (pro-rated entitlements)
- Contract staff (subject to employment terms)

### **1.3 Guiding Principles**

- Leave should be planned in advance whenever possible
- Operational needs must be considered
- Fair and consistent application across all employees

### **1.4 Definitions**

- **Working Day**: Any day the employee is scheduled to work
- **Immediate Family**: Spouse, children, parents, siblings
- **Calendar Year**: 1 January to 31 December

## **Page 2: Annual Leave Entitlements**

### **2.1 Annual Leave Entitlement**

#### **Years of Service Annual Leave Days**

< 1 year 10

1 – 2 years 12

3 – 5 years 15

\> 5 years 18

### **2.2 Pro-Rated Leave**

Employees who join mid-year will receive leave calculated as:

$$Annual~Leave = \frac{\text{Months Worked}}{12} \times \text{Entitlement}$$

### **2.3 Carry Forward Policy**

- Maximum of **5 days** can be carried forward
- Must be used within **first quarter of next year**
- Unused leave will lapse thereafter

### **2.4 Encashment**

- Leave encashment is **not allowed** unless:
  - o Upon resignation
  - o Approved by management

## **Page 3: Medical & Sick Leave**

### **3.1 Sick Leave Entitlement**

#### **Type of Leave Entitlement (Days)**

Outpatient Leave 14

Hospitalisation 60

### **3.2 Conditions for Sick Leave**

- Must be certified by a registered medical practitioner
- Medical certificate (MC) must be submitted within **48 hours**

### **3.3 Extended Illness**

Employees with prolonged illness may:

- Apply for unpaid leave
- Use annual leave balance
- Be assessed on a case-by-case basis

### **3.4 Medical Appointments**

- Time-off for medical appointments is allowed
- Must be scheduled outside working hours where possible

## **Page 4: Special Leave Types**

### **4.1 Maternity Leave**

- Female employees are entitled to **16 weeks** maternity leave
- Must have at least **3 months of service**

### **4.2 Paternity Leave**

- Male employees are entitled to **2 weeks** paternity leave
- Must be taken within **16 weeks of childbirth**

### **4.3 Compassionate Leave**

#### **Relationship Leave Entitlement**

Immediate Family 3 days

Extended Family 1 day

### **4.4 Marriage Leave**

- Employees are entitled to **3 days** of marriage leave
- Must be taken within the same calendar year

### **4.5 Study Leave**

- Granted at management discretion
- May be paid or unpaid

## **Page 5: Leave Application & Administration**

### **5.1 Application Process**

Employees must:

- 1. Submit leave via HR system
- 2. Obtain supervisor approval
- 3. Apply at least **7 days in advance** (where applicable)

### **5.2 Approval Guidelines**

Supervisors will consider:

- Workload and coverage
- Business needs
- Employee leave balance

### **5.3 Unpaid Leave**

- Granted only under exceptional circumstances
- Subject to management approval

### **5.4 Public Holidays**

Employees are entitled to all gazetted public holidays. If required to work:

- Replacement leave will be granted
- Or overtime compensation provided

### **5.5 Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually to ensure:

- Compliance with labour laws
- Alignment with company practices
